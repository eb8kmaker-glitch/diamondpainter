Quick Start

From Photo to Printed Pattern in 5 Steps

New to Diamond Painter? This visual guide walks you through the complete workflow — upload a photo, configure your canvas, generate the pattern, refine it, and export. No experience needed.

Upload

Upload Your Photo

Drag and drop any photo into the upload zone, or click to browse your device. JPG, PNG, and WebP are all supported. Your photo is processed entirely inside your browser — nothing is ever sent to a server.

💡 High-contrast photos with a clear subject work best — pet portraits, single-person portraits, and graphic designs all convert beautifully. Blurry or very dark photos can be trickier.

Configure

Configure Your Settings

Before generating, set three things in the left panel: bead type, canvas size, and color count. The grid size and total bead count update in real time so you can see the effect immediately.

⬤
Bead Type
Round (2.8mm) or Square (2.5mm). Round is easier for beginners.
📐
Canvas Size
Choose a cm-based preset or enter a custom size.
🎨
Color Count
5–80 DMC colors. More colors = more realistic but more materials needed.
💡 First time? Start with Round beads, 40×50 cm canvas, 35 colors. You can always change settings and regenerate instantly.

Generate

Generate Your Pattern

Click the Generate button. The tool runs k-means color clustering entirely in your browser — no waiting for a server. Most images complete in under 30 seconds. A progress indicator shows each processing stage.

⚡ The results card shows your grid dimensions (e.g. 143×178 beads), total bead count, and the actual finished size in centimeters — updated automatically when you change settings.
💡 Not happy with the result? Adjust the color count or canvas size and click Generate again. Each generation takes the same time — comparing 20, 35, and 50 colors takes about 90 seconds total.

Edit

Refine Your Pattern

The generated pattern is a strong starting point. Use the editing tools to fine-tune manually — fix a color in a specific area, replace an entire region, or merge two similar colors to simplify your palette. This step is optional but can significantly improve the result.

🖊
Paint
Click or drag to paint individual cells with the active color.
🪣
Fill
Replace an entire connected region of one color with another.
💉
Pick
Click any cell to select its color as the active painting color.
🗑
Erase
Remove manual edits, reverting cells to the original generated color.
💡 Check the DMC color stats panel on the right side of the canvas. It shows each color's bead count and percentage — useful for identifying colors used very sparingly (which can often be merged or removed).

Export

Export and Print

When you are happy with the pattern, choose your export format. Both options include the full pattern with cell symbols and a DMC color legend.

📤
Image Export
High-resolution PNG with the full pattern and legend. Good for printing at a copy shop or viewing on a tablet.
📄
PDF Export
The pattern is tiled across multiple A4 pages with a color legend at the end — ready to print at home.
💡 Label each printed PDF page (A1, A2, B1…) before assembly. The filename includes your canvas size, color count, and date for easy reference.
